What Your Favorite Writers’ Phones Look Like

Posted by Shane Jones @ 1:38 pm on October 24th, 2016

In 2013-2014 I did a series of intrusive posts on the lives of writers. These include asking what they eat for lunch, what their refrigerators look like, what music they listen to when they work out, and what their diets are like. Now HTMLGIANT is back and I haven’t matured. So I asked writers I admire to send me their phone homescreens. Many declined. Several sent a screenshot only to retract hours later for a variety of reasons (a pornographic background, their therapist said this was a bad idea, and “I’m trying to have a baby” were all reasons). One writer – well respected/award winning – had a flip phone. Regardless, I think this type of thing is light and fun and hopefully you will enjoy peeping these twenty phones as much as I did.

Vi Khi Nao is the author of Fish In Exile.

Annie DeWitt is the author of White Nights In Split Town City.

Elisa Albert is the author of After Birth.

Claire Donato is the author of Burial.

Chelsea Martin is the author of Mickey.

Chelsea Hodson is the author of Pity The Animal.

Ottessa Moshfegh is the author of Eileen (not sure what this is, but she sent it).

David Shields is the author of Reality Hunger.

Catherine Lacey is the author of Nobody Is Ever Missing.

Hannah Lillith Assadi is the author of Sonora.

James Frey is the author of A Million Little Pieces.

Alexandra Kleeman is the author of Intimations.

Molly Brodak is the author of Bandit.

Andrew Weatherhead is the author of Cats And Dogs.

Rachel B. Glaser is the author of Paulina & Fran.

Blake Butler is the author of 300,000,000.

Sarah Gerard is the author of Binary Star.

Sasha Fletcher is the author of Good Year.

April Ayers Lawson is the author of Virgin And Other Stories.

Adam Robinson is the publisher of Publishing Genius Press.

Porochista Khakpour is the author of The Last Illusion.

 Mark Baumer is the author of Holiday Meat.
