Faux Pool vol. 1: A Tenth of a Tear

timgrahamncanada, no. 27: Richard Prince, Clement Greenberg, Ryder Ripps

[1] I think most scholarship can be divided into pattern-matching ("connective") and analytical modes. Patternmatchers connect many phenomena in order to craft narratives and make arguments about underlying similarities. "Notes on Camp" is connective, as are Lasch's The True and Only Heaven and the writings of Maggie Nelson. I am primarily a patternmatcher. (The latter camp of scholarship, meanwhile, practices formal/historical analysis as an end in itself. Of course, the relationship between both camps is symbiotic: good, accurate patternmatching requires an understanding — and hence, good analysis — of matched phenomena; analysis gains big picture significance in part through patternmatching.)
