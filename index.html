Graham Johnson

Columbia University with a focus on American literature, music, and popular culture.

Interests: Rationalism, post-rationalism (?), metacrit., Trilling, Schmidhuber, 4chan, Ribbonfarm, pragmatism, counter-signaling, metamod, nostalgia, Weird Sun, RCs, NBA, Random Cloud.

Some things I'm proud of:

  • Developing a concept of critical proxies in music crit, as initially sketched out here and fully developed in the final section of this essay.

  • Terming and developing the concept of generic fitness, or generic "fit," as laid out in a post for Suspended Reason.

  • Helping start, and then running as managing editor, the first U.S. undergraduate journal of art criticism (JAC).

    Newsletter

    Once weekly, now-comatose newsletter with experimental computer artist Tim Goodwin and walking rock'n'roll encyclopedia/Art Forum fact-checker Canada Choate. We send (sent) letters out every Monday at 4pm.

    (Archive)

    Newsletter #14: Over the Hump, Some Flânerie, *nods*
    Graham writes on utopia, millennialism, temporal bandwidth, and the new survey showing that 78% of the American publishing industry identifies as female. Canada touches on Stockhausen and museum culture while Tim relays his experience with McLuhan's The Medium is the Message.

    Newsletter #13: [Untitled]
    Ben Hozie, frontman of the Bushwick art rock group Bodega Ben, a passionate defense of Wire's Pink Flag. Canada chronicles her first viewing of David Lynch's Eraserhead. Graham crowns The Big Short the worst film of the year, and the nuances of gendered power dynamics/structural privilege are explored in response to Scott Alexander's "Radicalizing the Romanceless."

    Newsletter #12: Hey, We're Back!
    Tim talks his trip to Vietnam and the tension between cultural authenticity and commerce in the tourist industry. Canada mentions the new indie film Carol, and that one time Jello Biafra from the Dead Kennedys ran for San Francisco mayor.

    Newsletter #11: Goodbye to David Bowie, Welcome to A (J) Guest
    Canada mourns the loss of David Bowie while Graham and guest contributor AJ Stoughton go at it in longform pieces arguing for subtlety, beauty, and a turn away from deconstruction. Tim continues his inquiry into the effects of lists and/or listicles on contemporary media consumption.

    Newsletter #10.1: Late Words, on Incompleteness
    Tim, stranded in Vietnam, sends out a supplementary newsletter discussing the Liar's Paradox and the book Gödel, Escher, Bach.

    Newsletter #10: (Untitled)
    Graham qualifies his previous dismissal of Didion and lists out his current RSS subscriptions with commentary. Canada finishes her long-awaited 80s playlist, tracking in at one hundred of the most important cuts of the decade.

    Newsletter #9: The Year in Review
    Year-end lists on books, films, music, and personal moments. Also, a retrospective on Jeb Bush's extraordinarily dry (and extraordinarily witty) sense of humor on the GOP Presidential trail.

    Newsletter #8: What kind of country should Our country be? (Xmas Newsletter)
    Canada talks her upcoming book on Ulrike Muller; Tim discusses the politics and Lord of the Flies behavior found in the Facebook group Post-Aesthetics. Graham lets loose more than he should have on why citizens need to stay open to dissenting political views.

    Newsletter #7: ugh but also other things
    Canada talks her post-punk dream girl radio show CONVICTION, Tim chats listicles, and Graham opts out for a celebratory margarita.

    Newsletter #6: A Weird Week
    Thanksgivings are discussed; Yale/Mizzou/Columbia activist activities drag on. Kim Gordon's annual Turner Prize is broken down and John McWhorter's ideas on linguistic appropriate get touched on briefly. A weird week indeed.

    Newsletter #5: Signal-to-Noise
    How to discern the good from the bad, especially when time and attention are Internet currencies, and the modern world produces more content daily than ever before in human history. Tim explains the science behind signal-to-noise (SNR), Graham riffs off potential reasons for why the media is at its most partisan point in over half a century. Canada and Graham bicker over the merit of Maggie Nelson's The Argonauts (an opinion the former will qualify when he actually reads the book).

    Newsletter #4: Cultures and Counter-Cultures // Com-part-men-ta-li-zation // Lucky to Be Here
    Discussed: How subcultures and communities operate (in-group, out-group dynamics; social signaling and identity politics), whether subtlety matters in art, automated classification systems, Ulrike Muller and the women of punk.
    "Low-stakes, uncritical ("postcritical") criticism is not just neutered and boring but also self-defeating; it invalidates itself with every mild line and qualifying 'maybe.'"

    Newsletter #3: Taxonomies, Monuments, Greater New York
    Dicussed: How best to taxonomize and geneologize culture, Yale & Missou activist uprisings, the Old New York, and how entropy and decay manifest in both physical and digital objects.

    Newsletter #2: Information! Transportation! Differentiation!
    Discussed: Whether poptimism is the new face of music elitism; how identity politics can sometimes interfere with "political politics"; information entropy in language; the Hi Fi Snock Uptown music venue's midlife crisis; art and religion.

    Newsletter #1: Ugliness, Sentimental Dirt, & the Old Masters
    Discussed: The Internet Ugly Aesthetic; the state of mp3 blogs; American Pharaoh winning the Grand Slam; Carrie Brownstein's new memoir; Frank Stella at the Whitney, Jack & Eliza impersonators. Also, vaporwave and the eradication of extreme poverty.
